Metadata quality comparison

The MSU assessed the six metadata elements above required for the minimum viable description of digitized items.

  • Green is a passing score
  • Yellow is a partial score (i.e. some but not all of the quality criteria were met)
  • Orange is a failing score (i.e. the element is not present at the item-level)

Why is this important?

This information provides a more granular analysis to help the MSU strategize and track targeted remediation projects. Improving metadata quality improves discovery of and context for our online collections.

Minimum mandatory element scoring criteria

title

  • Record has at least one title with text (Completeness)
  • Record has one title marked usage="primary" (Conformance)

typeOfResource

  • typeOfResource is present and matches an allowed value (Completeness)

identifier

  • Record has at least one identifier of type: "local_bnumber", "local_mss", or "local_tms" (Completeness)

genre

  • Record has at least one genre (Completeness)
  • Record has at least one genre with an authority attribute that is not blank (Accessibility)

date

  • Record has at least one numeric date (Completeness)
  • Record has at least one W3CDTF encoded date (Accessibility)

location

  • Record has Repository information (Completeness)
  • Record has Division information (Completeness)
  • Record has shelfLocator information (Completeness)
  • Record has multiple location elements with consistent Division values (Accuracy)
